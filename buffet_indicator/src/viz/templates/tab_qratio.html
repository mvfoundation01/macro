About Tobin's Q-Ratio

Tobin's Q-Ratio compares the market value of US nonfinancial corporate equity to the replacement cost of the underlying assets. Data comes from the Federal Reserve's Z.1 Financial Accounts release (quarterly, 1952+) and is forward-filled to monthly frequency. Q = 1 is theoretical equilibrium. Q > 1 means investors are paying a premium above replacement cost. The metric was popularized in modern markets by Andrew Smithers and was central to Smithers & Wright's Valuing Wall Street.
