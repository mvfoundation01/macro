About Crestmont P/E

Crestmont P/E (Easterling 2010, Probable Outcomes) is a cyclically-adjusted P/E that normalizes real price by a smooth exponential trend of real earnings, rather than Shiller's 10-year trailing average. Formally: crestmont_pet = real_pricet / exp(α̂ + β̂·t), where (α̂, β̂) are fit via OLS on log(real_eps) over the full sample.

Compared to CAPE, the Crestmont trend is smoother (insensitive to single-year earnings shocks) but more sensitive to the slope of the historical fit. The two indicators usually agree on direction; when they diverge, the gap is informative about whether recent earnings are above or below the long-run trend line.
