Interpretation

The pure credit risk premium: how much extra yield investors demand for junk bonds over investment-grade, with Treasury duration stripped out. Historically widens to >5pp during recessions and compresses below 2pp in late-cycle euphoria.

About this indicator

Source: Derived: BAMLH0A0HYM2 − BAMLC0A0CM (1996-12-present)

Direction convention: Pure credit risk premium — strips out the Treasury rate level. Trend convention: high spread = bearish equities.

Methodology: Daily series resampled to month-end. Z-score via expanding-window Huber on the canonical signal column. Predictive regression uses Hansen–Hodrick (1980) HAC standard errors as primary SE, with Newey–West (1987) as cross-check and Stambaugh (1999) bias correction for highly persistent regressors. OOS R² per Goyal–Welch (2008); Clark–West (2007) MSPE-adjusted statistic for nested-model comparison. Bootstrap CIs via Politis–Romano (1994) stationary bootstrap, 10,000 replications, seed=42. Sample-size penalty per master spec §6.2 when n_obs < 100.

References: Gilchrist & Zakrajšek (2012)
