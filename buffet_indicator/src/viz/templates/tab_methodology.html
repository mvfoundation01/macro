Methodology

Statistical framework, data hierarchy, and per-model implementation details behind every number on this dashboard.

Jump to: Data Framework Models Forward returns Probability Conviction Glossary

1. Data sources & hierarchy

The pipeline assembles a unified monthly panel from public economic data going back to 1871. Where multiple sources overlap, the data hierarchy preserves the longest series while deferring to higher-frequency, higher-quality vendor data where available:

  • S&P 500 price & total return — Shiller composite (1871-1989) spliced with Norgate Diamond total-return series (1990-present) where available; FRED SP500 + dividend-adjusted Yahoo ^GSPCTR otherwise.
  • CPI & real-dollar deflation — Shiller monthly CPI (1871-1949) → BLS CPI-U (1950-present).
  • GDP — Federal Reserve Z.1 nominal GDP, monthly via cubic-spline interpolation from quarterly observations.
  • Corporate equity & replacement cost — Federal Reserve Z.1 LM103164103 and LM102010005 (Tobin's Q numerator and denominator), 1952-present.
  • Wilshire 5000 & broad market cap — FRED WILL5000PRFC, 1971-present.
  • 10Y real yield — FRED DFII10 (TIPS, 2003-present); Cleveland Fed expected inflation × DGS10 for pre-2003 history.

1.1 Frequency & alignment

All series are aligned to month-end (last business day of each calendar month) before any statistical operation. Quarterly data is forward-filled inside the quarter. Each pipeline step preserves the alignment date; no series is computed with mismatched dates.

2. Statistical framework

2.1 Z-score construction

Each variant produces a level series (e.g. CAPE = price / 10-yr real EPS). The level series is standardized into a long-run z-score using a robust mean and Huber σ over the entire available history of that variant. The Huber σ uses scipy.stats.median_abs_deviation with a tuning constant of c = 1.345, which matches the asymptotic efficiency of the OLS estimator at the normal distribution.

The current_regime frame uses a Bai-Perron multiple-break detection model to identify the latest regime boundaries (max 5 breaks, BIC penalty); the z-score in this frame is standardized using only observations from the most recent regime.

2.2 Predictive regression

Forward-return regressions take the form r_{t+h} = α + β · z_t + ε_{t+h} where r_{t+h} is the annualized total return over the next h months. Inference uses Newey-West HAC standard errors with lag set equal to the horizon, controlling for the auto-correlation induced by overlapping observations.

The Stambaugh bias-correction is applied to β when the predictor z is highly persistent (ρ > 0.95). This corrects the small-sample bias that arises when the regressor is near-integrated and innovations to z are correlated with future returns.

2.3 Out-of-sample R2 (Goyal-Welch)

R2OOS is computed via expanding-window recursive estimation: at each date t, the model is fit on data through t-1 and used to predict t. The Goyal-Welch R2OOS compares the recursive-model SSE against the historical-mean SSE; positive values indicate genuine out-of-sample skill, negative values indicate the model underperforms a no-skill mean prediction.

3. Per-model implementations

3.1 Buffett Indicator (3 variants)

Market capitalization / GDP, computed in three variants that trade coverage breadth for series length:

  • bi_allequity_pct: Federal Reserve Z.1 total corporate equity (public + private), 1945+.
  • bi_wilshire_pct: Wilshire 5000 / GDP, 1971+ (cleaner market signal but shorter history).
  • bi_spx_proxy: S&P 500 / GDP, mean-matched to Wilshire on their common window; series back to 1871 via Shiller's spliced SPX.

3.2 CAPE (Shiller P/E10)

Real S&P 500 price divided by the trailing 10-year average of real EPS. Uses Shiller's monthly CPI for inflation adjustment. The metric is computed end-of-month for every month where the trailing 10-year window has ≥ 100 valid earnings observations.

3.3 Tobin's Q-Ratio

Market value of US nonfinancial corporate equity (Z.1 LM103164103) divided by the replacement-cost value of corporate assets (Z.1 LM102010005). Quarterly observations are forward-filled to month-end. Q = 1 is theoretical equilibrium; Q > 1 means investors are paying a premium above replacement cost.

3.4 Equity Yield Deficit

Real 10-year Treasury yield (DFII10 / Cleveland Fed proxy for pre-2003) minus the CAPE earnings yield (1 / CAPE × 100). Positive readings indicate bonds offer more real income than equities; negative readings indicate the opposite.

3.5 Mean Reversion

Real (CPI-adjusted) S&P 500 composite plotted against its long-run exponential trend (1871-present). The valuation reading is the percent deviation of current real price from the fitted trend; the z-score is standardized over the full history.

3.6 Crestmont P/E (v9.0)

Crestmont P/E (Easterling 2010): a cyclically-adjusted P/E that normalizes real price by a log-linear trend of real earnings rather than a trailing 10-year average. The trend is fit via log(real_epst) = α + β·t + ε over the full 1871-present sample, then crestmont_pet = real_pricet / exp(α̂ + β̂·t). Differs from Shiller CAPE in normalization basis (smooth exponential trend vs. moving average); both are valid normalizers, and Crestmont's smoothness reduces sensitivity to single-year earnings shocks. Note that when real-earnings growth tracks real-price growth over multi-decade periods, Crestmont becomes mathematically close to the Mean Reversion indicator.

3.7 Tactical backtest (v10.0)

The Backtest tab applies a simple binary rule using the MVCI z-score: 100% T-bills when z > +2σ, 100% equities when z < −1σ, 50/50 in between. Monthly rebalancing with a 10bps round-trip transaction cost charged only on weight changes greater than 1%. Look-ahead bias is prevented by lagging the signal one month — the weight in force during month t is computed from z observed at the end of month t−1 (verified by test_run_backtest_no_lookahead). Sharpe ratios are reported with stationary block bootstrap 95% confidence intervals (Politis-Romano 1994, mean block length 12 months, 10,000 reps). Risk-free rate is a short-rate proxy derived from the Shiller long-rate yield × 0.6, adjusted for realized inflation to produce a real monthly log return. This is a sufficiency check — multi-rule comparison with White's Reality Check (testing whether the best rule could have arisen by chance over many candidate rules) is deferred to v10.1.

3.8 MV Composite Index (MVCI)

A standardized aggregation of all variants above. Three weighting schemes:

  • equal_weight (default) — robust to over-fitting; each constituent contributes 1/N.
  • inv_variance — weights inversely proportional to constituent z-score variance over the common window.
  • pca_pc1 — projects constituent z-scores onto the first principal component; reports the explained-variance fraction.

4. Forward-return framework

Forward returns at horizon h months are computed as annualized total-return CAGR from the observation date to h months later. The pipeline computes returns for h ∈ {12, 24, 36, 60, 120} months. Predictive regressions report results separately at each horizon; the 120m (10-year) horizon is the headline.

The primary FR series is the spliced total-return series (Shiller × Norgate). Two robustness FR series are also computed: (a) price-return only and (b) Yahoo-only (no Norgate); cross-checks ensure key statistics are stable across all three.

5. Probability & CI machinery

Point probabilities (P(neg 10Y), P(< 5% CAGR), P(> 7% CAGR)) are computed from the OLS predictive regression's conditional distribution at the current z. Bootstrap 95% CIs use 5000 resampled-with-replacement OLS fits, with block bootstrap blocks sized equal to the horizon to preserve overlapping-observation correlation.

Bayesian posteriors use conjugate normal priors with weakly informative hyperparameters centred on historical averages and broad variance; posterior CIs reflect uncertainty about both β and the error variance.

6. Conviction score

The "Full Conviction" score (0-5) aggregates evidence quality and signal strength across five components:

  1. Signal strength — magnitude of current z-score relative to historical extremes.
  2. Statistical significance — Newey-West t-stat magnitude.
  3. In-sample fit — R2.
  4. Out-of-sample fit — Goyal-Welch R2OOS.
  5. Cross-variant agreement — fraction of constituent variants currently in the same regime.

Each component contributes a 0-1 score; the headline is the equally-weighted sum scaled to 0-5. The score is intended as a qualitative summary, not a precise forecast confidence.

7. Glossary

Z-score (σ)
Standardized deviation of an observation from the long-run mean, in units of long-run standard deviation. Z = 0 is the long-run mean; |z| = 1 is one σ away.
Huber σ
A robust scale estimator that is less sensitive to outliers than the sample standard deviation. Uses the median absolute deviation (MAD) with tuning constant c = 1.345.
Newey-West HAC SE
Standard errors that correct for both heteroskedasticity (changing variance) and autocorrelation (overlapping observations). Required for valid inference in predictive return regressions.
Stambaugh bias-correction
A small-sample correction for the upward bias in β when the predictor is highly persistent and its innovations are correlated with future returns. Stambaugh (1999, JFE).
Bai-Perron break test
A statistical procedure for detecting multiple unknown structural breaks in a time-series mean. Used in the "current_regime" frame to identify the latest regime boundary.
Goyal-Welch R2OOS
Out-of-sample R2 comparing a recursively-fit predictive model against a no-skill mean prediction. Positive values indicate genuine forecasting skill; negative values indicate the model underperforms a historical-mean benchmark.
Percentile rank
The fraction of historical monthly observations at or below the current reading. Reported as an integer percentile (0-100).
MVCI (MV Composite Index)
A composite z-score aggregating multiple constituent valuation indicators. Three weighting schemes: equal_weight (default), inv_variance, pca_pc1.
PCA-PC1 explained variance
The fraction of joint variation in the constituent z-scores captured by their first principal component. Values above 60% indicate a strong common latent valuation factor.
CAGR
Compound annual growth rate. The annualized rate of return that would produce the realized total return over a horizon.
