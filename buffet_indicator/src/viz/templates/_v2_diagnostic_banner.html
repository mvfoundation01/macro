V2 MV-Conditional rules — DIAGNOSTIC view only

We pre-registered three tactical de-risking rules (R-PRIMARY, R-ALT1, R-ALT2) that would deploy partial T-bill weight when MVCI and/or MRC valuation z-scores exceeded thresholds. The pre-registration commit (a90b02d, 2026-05-21) locked the rules BEFORE we ran the OOS test, and locked falsifiability criteria (Sharpe lift ≥ +0.05 OR MaxDD improvement ≥ 3pp).

All three rules failed the pre-registered falsifiability test:

  • R-PRIMARY (MVCI > +1.5σ AND MRC > +0.5σ): the +1.5σ MVCI threshold never fired in 2000–2026 (MVCI max in window = 1.43). V2 ≡ V1 mechanically. Sharpe lift = 0.000 → REJECTED.
  • R-ALT1 (MVCI > +2.0σ alone): also never fired. V2 ≡ V1. Sharpe lift = 0.000 → REJECTED.
  • R-ALT2 (continuous gradient): did fire but underperformed V1 (Sharpe diff = −0.013, Holm-Šidák p = 0.0077 in the wrong direction). REJECTED.

The most likely explanation: the modern monetary regime (post-2008 Fed accommodation) has compressed extreme MVCI readings compared to the pre-2000 sample our priors were based on. The thresholds were designed honestly but the OOS sample didn't contain any signals strong enough to fire R-PRIMARY/R-ALT1.
