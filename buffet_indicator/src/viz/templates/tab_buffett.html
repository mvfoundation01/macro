Notes on the three variants

All-Equity uses Federal Reserve Z.1 data covering all US corporate equity (public + private), 1945+. It is the broadest market-cap-to-GDP measure and Buffett's original framing.

Wilshire uses the Wilshire 5000 (the broadest US public equity index) divided by GDP, 1971+. Narrower coverage but a cleaner market signal.

SPX proxy uses the S&P 500 level divided by GDP, mean-matched to the Wilshire ratio over their common window. Useful for the deepest history (1871+ via the Shiller-spliced SPX series) but the absolute level is not a market-cap ratio — only its scale-invariant statistics (z, percentile) are valid valuation signals.
