About the Equity Yield Deficit

EY-Deficit = real 10Y Treasury yield − CAPE earnings yield (1/CAPE). Positive readings mean Treasuries offer more real income than equities; negative readings mean equities still earn more on a yield basis. The metric is the cross-asset opportunity cost missing from any equity-only indicator (CAPE, Q-Ratio, Buffett), and was historically very negative during ZIRP — a sign that real bonds simply could not compete with even richly-valued equities. Now that real bond yields are positive again, the deficit has narrowed sharply, making bonds an increasingly real alternative.
